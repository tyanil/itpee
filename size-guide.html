Size Guide

Finding the perfect fit is essential for comfort and foot health. Use our comprehensive size charts to find your ideal shoe size. If you're between sizes, we recommend ordering the larger size.

How to Measure Your Foot

  1. Place a piece of paper on a hard floor against a wall.
  2. Stand on the paper with your heel against the wall.
  3. Mark the longest part of your foot on the paper.
  4. Measure the distance from the wall to the mark in centimeters.
  5. Use our size charts below to find your size.

Pro Tip: Measure both feet and use the larger measurement, as most people have one foot slightly larger than the other.

Width Guide

We offer various width options for select styles to ensure the perfect fit:

For slender feet with a narrower heel and forefoot

Medium (M, R, or B)

Standard width that fits most people

Wide (W or D)

For feet that need extra room across the ball of the foot

Extra Wide (XW, EW, or EE)

Maximum width for those who need significant extra space

Brand-Specific Sizing Notes

Different brands may fit differently. Here are some brand-specific notes to help you find the perfect fit:

  • Athletic Brands: Many athletic brands run slightly small. Consider ordering a half size up.
  • European Brands: These often have a narrower fit. If you have wider feet, consider going up a half size.
  • Dress Shoes: Leather dress shoes may feel tight initially but will stretch with wear. For comfort from day one, consider ordering a half size up.
  • Boots: If you plan to wear thick socks with your boots, order a half size larger than your regular shoe size.

Common Sizing Questions

What if I'm between sizes?

If you're between sizes, we recommend sizing up for comfort. You can always add an insole if needed, but you can't make a too-small shoe bigger.

Do shoes stretch over time?

Most shoes will stretch slightly with wear, especially those made of natural materials like leather. However, you should never buy shoes that feel uncomfortable hoping they'll stretch enough to fit properly.

When should I measure my feet?

Measure your feet at the end of the day when they are at their largest due to natural swelling that occurs throughout the day.

How much toe room should I have?

You should have about a thumb's width (3/8" to 1/2") of space between your longest toe and the end of the shoe.
